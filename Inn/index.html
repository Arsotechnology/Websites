History of Property

     The most important person to inhabit the house at 14 North Aurora St.was the Rev. Henry Lay. The Rev. Lay was the first bishop of the diocese of Easton. He bought the land and premises from Robert Lloyd Tilghman and his sister Augusta. Mrs. Tilghman inherited the property from her mother. No doubt the Bishop added to the original structure. The Nickerson family next owned the house until John McDaniel bought it. While this family lived on the premises less than ten years, their name has been strongly identified with the house. In 1943 Anne Hyde Ingraham bought the property. She was the widow of Prentiss Ingraham, whose father wrote “The Land of Legendary Lore”, a delightful tale of Countyin the earlier days. This fine old Victorian house is now in use as one of Easton’s charming Bed and Breakfast's.

     The John S. McDaniel House is located at 14 North Aurora St. Easton, MD. This lovely Victorian home was built in 1865 and has been the home of many prominent Maryland families.

      The B&B is centrally located in the historic district of Easton and is very proud of its historic plaque.The building itself is representative of the prosperous Victorian era in Easton.It sports a tall octagon tower and spacious wrap around porch.All rooms are decorated with mix period furniture and modern amenities. Their are five guest rooms and one two-bedroom suite all with private baths.
